Research Methodology

This project uses a mixed-methods approach, combining qualitative data and quantitative data. It examines Netflix's programming strategies, exclusive partnerships, and global commissioning practices that contribute to the platform's global dominance. The research process involved several key steps:

  1. Data Collection:I used a publicly available dataset from the Academy Awards Database to gather information on Netflix's Oscar nominations and wins over time from 2014 to 2026. This data helped visualize a pattern in Netflix's recognition and attempts to establish itself in these high-status areas. Additionally, information was collected from industry-related articles and trade reporting from Forbes, Variety, and The Athletic to explore exclusive deals, commissioning trends and strategies to explain what these deals mean within the industry and possible concerns. Scholarly literature was gathered to establish historical context on media regulation and deregulation as well as provide foundation on Netflix and how they operate to further situate Netflix within a discussion of innovation and concerns. A clear pattern emerges within these sources that allows for the organization of three categories: Historical/ industrial context, platform strategies, and recognition.
  2. Analysis: I transferred data about Netflix's Oscar nominations and wins onto a google spreadsheet and then used the csv file to create visual analysis using Tableau Public that showcases the number of nominations over the years and the number of nominations versus number of wins through the years. I incorporated statistics from industry/trade articles to highlight the impact Netflix has on the industry. How individuals react to their business, what their statistics mean for the industry. Information was condensed to provide only the most important information about Netflix business deals.
  3. Intentional choices: To make this project manageable, I limited reserach to only focusing on Netflix and not any of its competitors or other studios. Additionally, rather than spending too much time reseraching Netflix and their interface and algorithm, Information on this was breifly mnetioned to help provide background on the top and set up their rise.

AI Usage

This project uses AI tools like ChatGPT and Claude to help with the design of the webpage. All research and information was conducted by the owner of this project. However, translating information onto a webpage was assisted by AI. I set the basic foundation of each page such as setting up the header and navigation system, but AI was able to take it further by establishing a “look” to the project. To reduce overwhelming the page with text, AI helped in creatively designing the look of this project. Choosing a style to resemble a Netflix interface, creating cards that expand with text when clicked on, or cards that light up when you hover over them, and overall creating a design that enhances the look and professionalism of this project. AI was not used for data collection or interpretation. All factual claims, statistics, and source interpretations were drawn directly from my sources and datasets. Using AI has allowed me the possibility to take my findings and present it in a way that is more interactive and engaging for the visitor. My own coding skills are not too advanced, so I am always learning with AI. It explains what is going wrong in my code and why. It effectively bridged the gap between my coding abilities and the outcome of what a more experienced developer might produce. I experimented with layouts, structures and features with assurance AI would be there to assist me with troubleshooting if needed or quickly revert back to my previous request. Through my experience, AI made me aware of how easy it is to lean too heavily on the tools. Because of that, I felt it was important to be transparent about the ways AI shaped the design and structure of the webpage, even if its contributions occurred behind the scenes.
